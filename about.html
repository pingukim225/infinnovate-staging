Our Story

How one leader's corporate experience became the next generation's competitive advantage.

The Beginning

From Corporate to Classroom

After 20+ years leading innovation at firms like Accenture, IBM, and Oaktree Capital, Jimmy Kim saw a pattern: the most successful professionals weren't the ones with the best grades — they were the ones who understood business, finance, and technology before everyone else.

The problem? Schools weren't teaching any of it. Students graduated knowing calculus but not how to balance a budget. They could write essays but couldn't pitch an idea. They memorized history but never learned how money works.

Infinnovate was born to close that gap. Based in Fullerton, California, our academy gives middle and high school students the real-world skills that school leaves out — through hands-on projects, expert mentorship, and a curriculum built from decades of actual business experience.

Our Mission

Believe in Being Infinite™

We believe every student has infinite potential — but potential without direction is just wasted talent. Infinnovate exists to bridge the gap between what students learn in school and what they actually need to succeed in the real world. Our mission is to equip the next generation with financial literacy, business leadership, and AI skills so they can build the future on their own terms.

Our Philosophy

The 6 Pillars of Growth

Every session, every project, and every interaction at Infinnovate is guided by these six principles.

1

Financial Fluency

Money management isn't optional — it's survival. We teach students to budget, save, invest, and build wealth from an early age.

2

Entrepreneurial Thinking

See problems as opportunities. Students learn to identify market gaps, create solutions, and take calculated risks with confidence.

3

Technology Mastery

AI, automation, and digital tools are reshaping every industry. We ensure students can build with technology, not just consume it.

4

Leadership & Communication

Great ideas die without great communication. Students practice pitching, presenting, and leading teams in every session.

5

Resilience & Adaptability

Failure isn't the enemy — staying down is. We create a safe space for students to fail, learn, iterate, and grow stronger.

6

Community & Impact

Success isn't just personal — it's shared. Students learn that the best businesses and leaders serve their communities first.
